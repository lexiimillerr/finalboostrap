Local Coffee Spots

Are you a coffee lover? If yes, then you are in the right place! Here you will be able to discover and explore some of Harrisonburg's best locally-owned coffee shops. We all need a break sometmes from your run-of-the-mill Dunkin or Starbucks.

Keep scrolling to learn more about Harrisonburg's top 5 coffee shops!

Greenberry's Coffee

After their first location in Charrlotsville, VA proved to be a success, entrapenuers Sean and Roxanne Simmons decided to bring their business to Harrisonburg. Not far from campus, the coffee shop is proud to serve its local JMU students and the Harrisonburg community. Over twnety-seven years later, Greenberry's continues to thrive and serve great coffee. When you visit, make sure to try their new, custom-made Nitro Cold Brew Coffee!

Visit Website

Black Sheep Coffee

Located in downtown Harrisonburg, Black Sheep Coffee opened its doors after owner and founder Chance Ebersold moved to Harrisonburg from Maine. The coffee shop features a variety of items such as locally-sourced coffee blends, pastries, and breakfast foods.

Visit Website

Broad Porch Coffee

Native to Luray, VA, this mostly wholesale coffee company specializes in keeping its customers stocked up on specially-sourced blends. Chose from its two locations and enjoy a great cup of coffee while getting a glimpse of the roastery process happening right in front of you! Broad Porch Coffee also provides opportunites for catering and renting the cafe for special events such as birthday brunches and anniversaries!

Visit Website

Chestnut Ridge Coffee

Harrisonburg's newest coffee shop, Chestnut Ridge Coffee, has only been around for five years but has since taken off thanks to locals Jose and David. Connected to Magpie's Diner, this coffee shop is the perfect place to hangout while enduring that diner line!

Visit Website

Merge Coffee Co.

Another fairly young coffee shop, Merge Coffee Co. prides itself in serving and supporting the Harrisonburg community. The idea of Merge came after two families decided to combine their love for coffee and start their own business.
